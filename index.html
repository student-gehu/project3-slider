ANIMAL
The elephant, with its imposing size and remarkable intelligence, holds a special place in both the natural world and human culture. As the largest land animal on Earth, elephants captivate our imagination with their majestic presence and intricate social structures. Comprising three species—the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant—these gentle giants inhabit a range of habitats, from the lush forests of central Africa to the arid plains of Asia. Their iconic features include the elongated trunk, which serves as a versatile tool for feeding, communication, and exploration, and the distinctive ivory tusks, tragically sought after by poachers.

ANIMAL
The cheetah is the fastest land animal, capable of reaching speeds of up to 60-70 miles per hour (96-112 kilometers per hour) in short bursts, making it a formidable predator on the African savannas and grasslands. Its sleek, aerodynamic body, long legs, and flexible spine enable it to accelerate and maneuver swiftly while chasing down its prey.
